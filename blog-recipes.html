May 2020 Daily Diet

To recover from kidney disease, I was put on a powerful, life-saving corticosteriod in October 2019. I was vaguely warned about some possible symptoms that might occur but didn't fully grasp the consequences of eating like a regular 21 year old until it was too late: intense cravings, increased appetite, water retension, higher blood sugar, and of course... weight gain. Weight gain that gave me painful stretch marks. Weight gain that gave me a beer belly, a moon face, a buffalo hump. Very uncomfortable and occassionally devasting to a 21-year old young woman. Kids, tell your docs to be real with you... and then Google the treatments they prescribe.

Now that I'm finally tapering off (slowly reducing my medication because my kidneys are doing better!), I wanted to create a diet that would help me lose weight while giving me all the nutrients I need (and none of the ones I really don't). I have one serving of each of the following four recipes every day, spaced about 3 hours apart to prevent cravings. Here are the key points of this diet:

  • Low salt
  • No added sugar
  • Low carb
  • Low "bad" cholesterol
  • Low triglycerides
  • Some good cholesterol and antioxidants
  • Lactose and gluten free
  • Lots of veggies and fruits!
  • Under 1000 calories a day

Vegetable Medley

Tell Me About It

A great and tasty way to get in your veggies! Beware of using too much black beans though (they're my favorite)-- they'll turn everything black!

Health Benefits

  • No cholesterol!
  • Low salt
  • Nutrient rich!
  • Under 300 calories per serving
  • Gluten Free

Green Smoothie

Tell Me About It

I constructed this recipe to get more greens in my diet while keeping my favorite fruits to eat every day. I never had a problem with the taste of raw kale, so I don't have any problems with it in my smoothie (unless it's not well blended enough)! The rolled oats (and the pinch of sliced almonds I add sometimes) add a nice, rough texture that I adore but you may not. Feel free to sub in blueberries for the strawberries.

Health Benefits

  • No added sugar
  • Low fat
  • Super greens and yummy fruits
  • Nutrient rich!
  • Antioxidants
  • Under 300 calories per serving
  • Lactose Free
  • Gluten Free
  • Vegan

Caldo

Tell Me About It

Caldo (soup) is such an easy recipe to make in bulk-- while still being healthy and cheap! Add and remove ingredients at your liking and as they are available.

Health Benefits

  • Low "bad" cholesterol
  • Low salt
  • Less than 300 calories per serving
  • Gluten Free

Egg Avocado Tacos

Tell Me About It

A very simple but reliable breakfast. I have no problem eating this every morning for a year straight (I know 'cus I've done it!)

Health Benefits

  • Low "bad" cholesterol (remove egg yolks)
  • High in "good" cholesterol (the avocado)
  • Low salt
  • Less than 300 calories per serving
  • Gluten Free

A More... "Regular" Diet

Now, I don't believe in diets. I believe the eating/food changes you make in your life should be lifestyle changes-- something you are willing to do for the rest of your life. With that said, once I reach my desired weight, I'll still be making those recipes from above. However, I'll likely be changing what I eat every week with one of the following:

Smoothies

Tell Me About It

Let's get more creative with our smoothies! Actually, the first one is especially healthy (has less sugar and added nutrients from the sweet potato). Now, I hate sweet potatoes buuuut this tastes like a pumpkin pie! I swear! The other smoothies are just plain, tasty and a fun mix-n-match sort of deal.

Health Benefits

  • Low "bad" cholesterol
  • No salt
  • One (or more) fruit and one veg serving per smoothie
  • Natural sugars

Tacos and Taco Bowls

Tell Me About It

I am very guilty for making this recipe way too many times in a given month-- it's just so easy and cheap, I can't help it! I love the fresh taste of the lettuce and salsa, and packing it all in a little bowl or wrapped in foil is irresistible to me.

Health Benefits

  • Low "bad" cholesterol
  • Low salt
  • High in protein
  • Whole veggies

Taco Soup

Tell Me About It

The inspiration for my Caldo recipe. Needless to say, it is ridiculously easy and cheap to make. You can also add and remove ingredients at will without taking away from its epicness.

Health Benefits

  • Low "bad" cholesterol
  • Low salt
  • High in protein

Turkey and Butternut Squash Chili

Tell Me About It

Another recipe I could make every other week and be perfectly happy. It's a one-pot recipe that you can make as thick or thin as you please. Serving with brown rice, some french bread or corn chips is ideal, but it's hefty enough to be eaten on its own.

Health Benefits

  • Low "bad" cholesterol
  • Low salt
  • High in protein

Sweet and Spicy Tempeh Bowls

Tell Me About It

Something I learned pretty fast entering college: meat is expensive. Something I learned recently: tofu is CHEAP. I love the idea of eating less meat (which is pro-kindness, pro-environment, and pro-your-own-health!). This is not an easy recipe for me to make, but oh goodness, is it filling and delicious.

Health Benefits

  • Low "bad" cholesterol
  • High in protein

Tell Me About It

I haven't made this recipe too many times, but it sure was memorable. The crunch of the water-chestnuts and the savoriness of the sauce and the coolness of the lettuce-- it all comes together beautifully.
